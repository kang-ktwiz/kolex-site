Your Complete Guide to Korean Sports Cheerleading

KL
KOLEX Editorial
May 25, 2026 · 8 min read

Korean sports cheerleading is one of the most distinctive performance traditions in global sports entertainment. If you're encountering it for the first time — perhaps through a social media clip, a streaming broadcast, or a friend's recommendation — this guide will give you everything you need to understand what you're watching and why it's worth following closely.

What Is Korean Sports Cheerleading?

Unlike American cheerleading (which emphasizes gymnastics and sideline chants) or Japanese cheerleading (which tends toward precise, choreographed group formations), Korean sports cheerleading blends professional dance performance with live crowd management. Performers lead audience participation sequences — chants, dances, call-and-response rituals — while delivering high-energy choreography that works both as live spectacle and as recorded content.

It operates across multiple professional sports leagues, primarily the KBO (Korean Baseball Organization), the KBL (Korean Basketball League), and the V-League (volleyball). Each league brings different venue sizes, pacing, and audience expectations.

How Does It Work?

A typical KBO cheerleader squad positions itself in the outfield section, facing the home fans. During at-bats, they lead specific chants for each player — every KBO team has a catalogue of player songs that fans know by heart. Between innings, the squad performs choreographed routines. During key moments (comeback situations, home run celebrations), they coordinate massive audience participation sequences that can involve 20,000+ people moving in unison.

Where Can I Watch?

Live: KBO games stream internationally via various platforms depending on your region. KBL games are similarly available. KOLEX performers' Instagram accounts are the best source for highlight clips and behind-the-scenes content between games.

Recorded: YouTube is rich with full-inning compilation videos, performance highlight reels, and fan-produced content. Searching individual performer names in Korean (e.g., "김하은 치어리더") returns substantial results.
