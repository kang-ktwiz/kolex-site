Korean Cheerleading: A Guide for Taiwanese Fans

KL
KOLEX Editorial
May 22, 2026 · 5 min read

Taiwan has a passionate sports culture and a long-standing appreciation for Korean entertainment — and that combination has produced one of the most engaged international audiences for KOLEX Sports Entertainment. Taiwanese fans have discovered Korean sports cheerleaders through a variety of pathways: K-drama fandom, K-pop communities, baseball enthusiasts who follow both CPBL and KBO, and social media clip culture. Wherever they started, they stayed.

Baseball Culture Bridges the Gap

Taiwan has a strong baseball tradition through the CPBL (Chinese Professional Baseball League), and Taiwanese baseball fans who began watching KBO — attracted by the competitive level and broadcast availability — quickly encountered KBO's distinctive stadium entertainment culture. The cheerleader dimension surprised many first-time KBO viewers: the scale of the coordinated audience participation, the quality of the individual performers, and the overall production value are genuinely different from what Taiwanese fans were accustomed to seeing in domestic games.

Instagram is the primary platform for following KOLEX performers. All twelve members post regularly during the active sports season.
