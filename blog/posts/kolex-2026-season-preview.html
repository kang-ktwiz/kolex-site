KOLEX 2026: What the Season Holds

KL
KOLEX Editorial
May 21, 2026 · 4 min read

The 2026 sports season is in full swing, and KOLEX Sports Entertainment is delivering on every front. Baseball season opened in late March with the KBO's 44th campaign, and the basketball and volleyball seasons have kept KOLEX performers active throughout. Here's what fans should know heading into the heart of the 2026 schedule.

A Deep Roster Operating at Full Strength

One of the most encouraging aspects of KOLEX's 2026 season is the depth and health of the squad. All twelve members are active, with no significant absences affecting the lineup. That's not always the case in a 162-game baseball season combined with basketball and volleyball commitments — the schedule is genuinely demanding — but 2026 has seen KOLEX performing consistently across all venues.

Young Members Are Stepping Up

The 2026 season has been notable for the emergence of KOLEX's younger members. Lee Seo-yun (born 2006) and Kim Min-ji (born 2005) are in their first full seasons at senior level, and both have shown the kind of comfort and authority that suggests they'll be central figures in KOLEX's identity for years to come. Watching them work alongside veterans like team captain Shin Se-hee is a master class in how strong squad cultures develop talent.

Social Media Milestones

Kim Hae-ri's ongoing approach to the 600K Instagram follower milestone is the season's most-watched individual storyline. She entered 2026 as the most-followed active Korean sports cheerleader and has continued to grow her audience. Her content strategy — a consistent mix of performance clips, preparation content, and personal moments — is a model for how the genre's top performers build and maintain international audiences.

The Schedule Ahead

The KBO season runs through October, giving fans months of regular-season action and then playoffs. The KBL season will restart in October as well, ensuring year-round KOLEX activity. For international fans in different time zones, social media remains the most practical way to follow — performance clips typically appear on Instagram within hours of the games.
